    New Member

    Yuhan (Douglas) Rao is from the North Carolina State University’s North Carolina Institute for Climate Studies (NCICS) in Asheville. His current research at NCICS focuses on generating a blended near-surface air temperature dataset by integrating in situ measurements and satellite observations via innovative statistical models. He is familiar with climate and weather foundation models.

    Official Plans

    Foundation Models

    • Geoffrey gave a short presentation AI4Science Foundation Models noting possible different scopes for foundation models.
    • Yuhan noted that there is a recent established collaboration between NASA and IBM on foundational model with satellite image - https://www.earthdata.nasa.gov/news/nasa-ibm-ai-collaboration
    • Wes noted that much big data has a low fraction of interesting data
    • Gregg put forward AlphaFold as a foundation model for science.
    • Yuhan noted Special issue – Benchmark datasets and machine learning algorithms for Earth system science data (ESSD/GMD inter-journal SI)
    • Also, we have our next NOAA AI Workshop (virtual) in September with focused discussion on benchmarking framework (5th NOAA AI Workshop (2023) | National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration).
    • Christine suggested linking to NSF Initiative in knowledge graphs led by Chaitan Baru. who is a Senior Advisor in NSF's new Technology, Innovation, and Partnerships (TIP)
    • Christine asked if we could apply to the GEO POSE? Pathways to Enable Open-Source Ecosystems (POSE) | NSF - National Science Foundation
    • Gregg Barrett noted that Feiyi Wang and Jeyan Thiyagalingam are hosting submissions on Foundational Models and Workflows in AI for Science | Frontiers Research Topic
    • If we have an initiative in Foundation models we need define a framework and have some prototypes – possibly small
